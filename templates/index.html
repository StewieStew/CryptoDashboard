1. System Overview — How Everything Works Together

This is a fully automated crypto analysis and paper-trading system. Every 5 minutes, a background scanner checks 13 coins across two timeframes, runs 9 independent technical checks, applies 6 layers of filtering, consults Claude AI, and decides whether to log a trade. Here is the exact sequence from market data to open position.

2. The 9 Signal Factors

Nine independent checks are run on every scan. Three are structural (2 pts each, maximum weight). Six are confirmation (1 pt each). Adaptive learning adjusts the weight of each factor over time based on its real win rate — a factor that keeps appearing on losing trades gets reduced; one that appears on winners gets boosted.

Factor 1 — Market Regime (default 2 pts)
HTF used: 15m → 1H regime | 4H → 1D regime Bullish: HTF price ABOVE 200-period EMA (institutional long bias) Bearish: HTF price BELOW 200-period EMA (institutional short bias) Also checks: HH/HL (uptrend) vs LH/LL (downtrend) structure on HTF

The regime is always evaluated on the higher timeframe — never the chart you're trading. A 15m long signal only has regime support if the 1H is above its 200 EMA. This is the most important filter: trading against the macro trend is the #1 cause of BOS failures. When regime aligns, institutional money is flowing in the same direction as your trade.

Factor 2 — Break of Structure / BOS (default 2 pts)
Swing detection window: 15m→3 bars, 4H→5 bars each side of pivot Bullish BOS: Current Close > Last confirmed Swing High → Buyers overpowered the last supply zone. Structural shift. Bearish BOS: Current Close < Last confirmed Swing Low → Sellers overpowered the last demand zone. Structural shift. BOS is REQUIRED for a signal — score alone cannot fire a trade

The BOS is the directional verdict. Without it, any move is just noise inside the existing range. The BOS confirms that one side of the market has taken decisive control, not just briefly pierced a level. Note: the system only accepts BOS via close, not a wick — a candle that briefly prints above the swing high then closes back below is NOT a BOS. This eliminates a major class of false breakout signals.

Factor 3 — Liquidity Sweep (default 2 pts)
Bullish Sweep: Low[i] < Swing Low AND Close[i] > Swing Low → Wick pierced below, candle closed above (stops taken, bulls absorbed) Bearish Sweep: High[i] > Swing High AND Close[i] < Swing High → Wick pierced above, candle closed below (stops taken, bears absorbed) Checked against last 4 swing pivots within the last 12 bars

Stop-loss orders cluster below swing lows (retail longs) and above swing highs (retail shorts). A sweep is when institutional players briefly push price through those clusters — triggering all the stops, buying their liquidity — then reverse hard. The trapped traders' forced exits (margin calls, stop-outs) then fuel the new move. A sweep + BOS in sequence is the highest-probability setup in the system.

Factor 4 — Volume Expansion (default 1 pt)
Expanding = Avg(Volume last 5 bars) > Avg(Volume bars −20 to −5) Spike = Any bar in last 10 has Volume > 2× Avg(last 20 bars) → Either condition earns the point

Volume confirms institutional participation. A breakout on low volume is a warning — the move may reverse when volume returns. A BOS on expanding volume or a volume spike means real money is behind the move. Without volume confirmation, the signal still fires if other factors align, but the post-trade AI gives it lower confidence.

Factor 5 — OBV Direction (default 1 pt)
OBV = running sum: +Volume on up-close bars, −Volume on down-close bars Bullish OBV: OBV trending up (20-bar EMA of OBV rising) Bearish OBV: OBV trending down Bullish Divergence: Price down 20 bars but OBV flat/up → hidden accumulation Bearish Divergence: Price up 20 bars but OBV flat/down → hidden distribution → Any aligned condition earns the point

OBV reveals smart-money flow that's invisible on price alone. A slow, quiet accumulation shows up in OBV before price breaks out. The OBV chart panel (below the main chart) lets you visually confirm whether volume flow is leading or lagging price. Divergence between OBV and price is an early warning that the current trend is running on empty.

Factor 6 — RSI Confirmation (default 1 pt)
RSI = 14-period exponential RSI on closing prices Bullish conditions (any one earns the point): • RSI > 60 in an uptrend regime (momentum aligned) • RSI was ≤30 in last 8 bars AND now > 30 (reset from oversold) Bearish conditions (any one earns the point): • RSI < 40 in a downtrend regime (momentum aligned) • RSI was ≥70 in last 8 bars AND now < 70 (reset from overbought)

RSI in the 60–100 "bullish range" during an uptrend means momentum is healthy and continuous — not a fade. A reset from oversold (30→31) is more actionable than RSI just sitting at 30, because it shows the selling wave is exhausting and buyers are stepping in. Strong trends often hold RSI above 50 for weeks without touching 30.

Factor 7 — MACD Confirmation (default 1 pt)
MACD Line = EMA(12) − EMA(26) of closing prices Signal Line = EMA(9) of MACD Line Histogram = MACD Line − Signal Line Bullish: MACD Line > Signal Line AND Histogram > 0 Bearish: MACD Line < Signal Line AND Histogram < 0 Bonus: Bullish/bearish crossover in last 3 bars = stronger confirmation

MACD measures momentum by comparing fast and slow exponential moving averages. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, short-term momentum has turned bullish. The histogram shows the acceleration: growing bars = momentum building, shrinking bars = momentum fading. MACD aligns with BOS direction to confirm the breakout has momentum, not just price displacement.

Factor 8 — ADX Trend Strength (default 1 pt)
ADX = 14-period Average Directional Index (measures trend strength, not direction) DI+ = Directional Indicator Plus (buying pressure) DI− = Directional Indicator Minus (selling pressure) ADX > 25 → trending market, signals are reliable → earns the point ADX < 25 → ranging/choppy, signal carry lower win rate → no point ADX > 50 → strong trend, highest reliability

ADX is the only factor that measures trend strength without a direction. A BOS in a ranging market (ADX < 25) is much more likely to be a false break than a BOS where ADX is 35+. When ADX is below 25, the system still fires if score is met — but the AI flags lower confidence and the Discord alert notes "ranging market." Over time, if ranging-market signals keep losing, the adaptive engine reduces this factor's weight.

Factor 9 — VWAP Alignment (default 1 pt)
VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / Σ(Volume) — rolling from session open Long signal: Current price > VWAP → price above fair value baseline Short signal: Current price < VWAP → price below fair value baseline

VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price) is the true average price paid by all participants weighted by volume. It's the benchmark institutional desks and algorithms use as a "fair value" anchor. Price trading above VWAP means buyers are in control intraday and willing to pay above average. For a long signal, being above VWAP confirms the directional bias has institutional flow behind it. For swing trades on 4H, VWAP acts as a multi-day trend anchor.

3. Confluence Score

All 9 factors are weighted and summed into a single score. The maximum varies as the adaptive engine adjusts individual weights, but starts at ~12 pts. A signal requires a minimum score (default 7.0, adaptive) AND a confirmed BOS. The weights are not fixed — they change automatically based on each factor's real win-rate performance.

Adaptive Weights — How They Change

After every 8 closed trades, the adaptive engine reviews each factor's win rate (among trades where that factor was present). If a factor is present on >70% wins → its weight increases up to 1.5× default. If it's present on <40% wins → weight decreases to as low as 0.3× default. This means the score maximum can range roughly from 8 to 18 depending on which factors the market is rewarding. The /api/learning endpoint shows all current weights live.

4. Signal Gating — 6 Layers Before a Trade Is Logged

A high confluence score is necessary but not sufficient. Every candidate signal passes through six independent filters in sequence. If any filter fails, the signal is discarded completely — no partial logging, no "watch" state. This multi-layer approach is why the system only logs high-quality setups.

Gate 1 — Adaptive Score Threshold
Signal fires only if: Confluence Score ≥ Signal Threshold Default threshold: 7.0 (adaptive — engine adjusts this over time) Threshold raises: when overall win rate < 40% in last 8 trades (+0.5) when 3+ consecutive losses in a row (+0.5, streak protection) Threshold lowers: when overall win rate > 70% in last 8 trades (−0.25) Range: 6.5 minimum to 9.5 maximum

The threshold is the system's confidence dial. After a losing streak it automatically becomes more selective — requiring stronger setups. After consistent winning it relaxes slightly to capture more opportunities. This means the system learns which market conditions deserve caution and which allow more aggressive scanning.

Gate 2 — BOS Confirmation (Hard Gate)
REQUIRED: Bullish BOS for LONG signals Bearish BOS for SHORT signals No BOS = no signal, regardless of score

No amount of indicator confluence can substitute for a structural break. A high score without BOS means many factors are aligned but price hasn't actually committed to a direction yet. The system waits until price proves it — the BOS is the trigger, not the setup.

Gate 3 — Tier-Based R:R Minimum
Day tier (15m, 30m): R:R must be ≥ 2:1 Swing tier (1h, 4h, 1d, 1w): R:R must be ≥ 3:1 R:R = |Target − Entry| / |Entry − Stop| If structural target is too close: → Target is projected to exactly the minimum R:R from entry → target_basis shows "3:1 R:R projection (structural target too close)"

At 3:1 R:R, the system only needs a 25% win rate to break even (each win is worth 3 losses). This means you can be wrong on 3 of every 4 swing trades and still not lose money. The system enforces this mathematically — if the nearest structural target only offers 2:1, the target is extended to 3:1. When the dashboard shows a 3:1 target, it may be a projected level rather than a named structural zone.

Gate 4 — Higher-Timeframe Bias Filter
15m LONG → passes only if 1H is BULLISH (above 200 EMA OR bullish BOS on 1H) 15m SHORT → passes only if 1H is BEARISH (below 200 EMA OR bearish BOS on 1H) 4H LONG → passes only if 1D is BULLISH 4H SHORT → passes only if 1D is BEARISH

Even if the 15m looks perfect, a LONG signal into a 1H downtrend is fighting the dominant order flow. This filter eliminates counter-trend trades at the structural level — not just at the EMA level. "Bullish" on the higher TF means either price is above its 200 EMA, or a bullish BOS was recently confirmed on that TF.

Gate 5 — Session Filter (Day Tier Only)
15m signals: only logged between 07:00 and 22:00 UTC → Covers European open (07:00) through US close (22:00) → Avoids Asia-only sessions where volume is thin and fakeouts are high 4H signals: fire any time — 24 hours, 7 days

Crypto doesn't close but it has session personalities. The highest quality intraday moves happen when European and US markets overlap (12:00–17:00 UTC is peak liquidity). Signals fired during Asian-only hours on the 15m tend to be low-volume, easily manipulated moves that reverse on the European open. The session filter keeps Day trades in high-liquidity windows.

Gate 6 — Claude AI Pre-Trade Veto
Input to Claude Haiku: signal score, R:R, entry/target/stop, direction, ADX, VWAP side, funding rate, BTC dominance, Fear & Greed index, system win rate, per-symbol win rate, Claude's own past accuracy Output: { confidence: 0-100, recommendation: "strong_take"|"take"|"skip" } → "skip": trade is VETOED — not logged, no Discord alert → "take": trade logged, AI badge shown as ✅ → "strong_take": trade logged, AI badge shown as 🔥 → API unavailable: fallback — trade logged without AI annotation

Claude evaluates the full context picture: is the Fear & Greed index showing extreme greed while taking a long? Are funding rates overheated? Is BTC making a major opposing move? These contextual factors aren't in any indicator — Claude synthesizes them with the technical picture and makes a go/no-go call. Critically, Claude also sees its own past accuracy (e.g. "my strong_take signals have a 68% win rate") and self-calibrates whether to be more or less aggressive.

5. Two-Tier Trade System

The scanner runs two completely separate tiers simultaneously. Each tier has different timeframes, session rules, R:R requirements, and maximum trade duration. The tier separation prevents the same signal from being double-counted — you can have one Day trade and one Swing trade open on the same symbol at the same time.

Opposing Signal Rule
If a LONG is open on 4H BTCUSDT and a new SHORT signal fires on 4H BTCUSDT: → The existing LONG is closed at entry price (cancelled, 0% ROI) → The new SHORT is opened This only triggers within the same tier. A 15m SHORT does not close a 4H LONG.

When the system fires a signal in the opposite direction of an existing trade on the same tier, it means the technical picture has completely reversed. Rather than holding a trade that the system no longer believes in, the old position is cancelled and the new thesis is opened. The entry price is used as the cancel price to avoid manufacturing a loss on what may have been a valid trade that simply reversed.

6. Trade Management

Once a trade is logged, the system manages it automatically on every 5-minute scanner sweep. Three mechanisms protect open positions: a partial profit lock at 1.5R, a breakeven trailing stop at 1R, and a maximum age expiry. No manual intervention is required.

Partial TP System — 1.5R Profit Lock
Partial TP level (for LONG) = Entry + 1.5 × (Entry − Stop) Partial TP level (for SHORT) = Entry − 1.5 × (Entry − Stop) When price hits the Partial TP: 1. 50% of position is considered booked at Partial TP price 2. Stop Loss → Entry (breakeven activated) 3. Discord "🔒 Partial TP Hit" alert sent When trade fully closes (after partial was hit): Case A — Full TP reached: ROI = 0.5 × partial_roi + 0.5 × full_tp_roi Case B — Breakeven hit: ROI = 0.5 × partial_roi + 0 = half the partial gain Status = WIN (still net profitable) Case C — SL somehow hit: ROI = 0.5 × partial_roi + 0.5 × stop_roi

The partial TP transforms many "break-even cancels" into small wins. Example: BTC trade, Entry $95,000, Stop $93,500 (risk = $1,500), Partial TP $97,250 (1.5R), Full TP $99,500 (3R). If price hits $97,250 then reverses to entry: you still made +$1,250 per BTC on the half position — a win. Without the partial, this would have been a 0% cancelled trade. Over many trades, this materially improves the win rate and average ROI.

Breakeven Trailing Stop — 1R Activation
Breakeven activates when price moves 1× risk distance in favour: LONG: current_price ≥ Entry + (Entry − Stop) SHORT: current_price ≤ Entry − (Entry − Stop) After activation: effective SL = Entry price → If price returns to entry → trade closes at 0% ROI (cancelled) → Guaranteed no loss once 1R is reached

The breakeven stop is a built-in loss prevention mechanism. Once the trade is 1R in profit, the stop permanently moves to entry. This creates a "free trade" scenario — the worst case from that point is breaking even. Note: the partial TP fires at 1.5R, which is after the breakeven would normally activate at 1R. Both happen automatically on the same scan cycle if price jumps past both levels in one move.

Target and Stop Levels
LONG target priority: 1. Prior swing high above current price 2. 200 EMA above current price (dynamic resistance) 3. 50 EMA above current price 4. Current price + 2.5×ATR (fallback projection) → If structural target gives < min R:R → projected to exactly min R:R LONG stop: Last confirmed swing high − (stop_multiplier × ATR) → ATR buffer absorbs noise/wicks, stop placed just below broken resistance → Max stop: 25% of entry price (hard cap)

Stops are placed at structurally meaningful levels, not arbitrary percentages. The stop belongs just below the level that, if reclaimed by sellers, would prove the bullish BOS was a false breakout. The adaptive stop multiplier (default 0.5×ATR) widens automatically if the system detects that stops are being hit by normal candle noise — a pattern that indicates the stop buffer is too tight for current volatility.

Maximum Age Expiry
Day trades (15m): expire after 72 hours if TP/SL not hit Swing trades (4h): expire after 14 days if TP/SL not hit → Closed at entry price → 0% ROI, status: cancelled

A trade that hasn't resolved after 72 hours (Day) or 14 days (Swing) has failed to follow through. Rather than holding it indefinitely, the system cancels it at entry — no loss, but the capital is freed. This prevents the trade log from filling with zombie positions that the original BOS has long since invalidated by market action.

7. Claude AI

Claude Haiku (fast, low-latency) evaluates every signal before it's logged. Claude Sonnet (deep analysis) reviews full trade history on demand. Both models receive the complete technical picture plus market context — not just indicator values, but interpretation of what those values mean together. Claude also tracks its own prediction accuracy and adjusts its criteria over time.

Pre-Trade Signal Evaluation (Haiku)
Inputs: Technical: score, R:R, entry/target/stop, ADX, VWAP side Market context: Fear & Greed index, BTC dominance, funding rate, open interest, session conditions Track record: system win rate, per-symbol recent wins, Claude's own historical accuracy by recommendation type Output JSON: confidence: 0–100 (Claude's conviction level) recommendation: "strong_take" | "take" | "skip" reasoning: 2-sentence explanation risks: [ list of specific risks ] positives: [ list of specific positives ] "skip" → trade vetoed completely, never logged

Claude doesn't just check boxes — it synthesizes. A score of 8 with extreme greed (F&G = 90) and overheated funding rates gets treated differently than a score of 8 in neutral market conditions. A technically clean setup in a symbol that has a poor recent win record gets flagged. This contextual layer is what makes the AI veto meaningful rather than just another indicator.

Self-Calibration — Claude Learns Its Own Accuracy
Before each evaluation, Claude receives: "strong_take: 12 trades → 9W/3L (75% win rate, avg ROI +3.2%)" "take: 8 trades → 4W/4L (50% win rate, avg ROI +0.1%)" Built-in calibration rules: strong_take win rate < 55% → raise the bar for strong_take take win rate < 45% → tighten criteria, skip more borderline setups Both > 65% → trust conviction signals more

This is the self-improvement loop. Claude doesn't just evaluate trades in isolation — it knows whether its past "strong_take" calls actually won. If its conviction calls have been right 75% of the time, it's more confident in future strong signals. If its "take" calls have only won 40%, it starts being more critical before recommending "take." No human adjustment needed — the feedback loop is automatic.

On-Demand Chart Analysis (Haiku)
Triggered: "Ask Claude ↻" button in the AI card Input: all 9 indicator values for current symbol + timeframe Output: { bias: LONG|SHORT|NEUTRAL, confidence: 0-100, summary: "2-sentence TA read", watch_for: "key trigger to confirm or invalidate", key_levels: ["price: what it means"], risks: ["specific risks"] }

This is separate from the trade signal evaluation. You can ask Claude for a chart read at any time, even when no signal is active. Claude gives its directional bias and confidence based purely on the current technical state — useful for understanding whether to watch a coin for a developing setup or to stay away from it entirely.

Performance Review (Sonnet)
Triggered: /api/ai-insights endpoint (requires ≥10 closed trades) Input: full closed trade history with factors_snapshot per trade, per-symbol breakdown, factor win rates, Claude's own accuracy Output: { overall_assessment, patterns, recommendations, watch_out }

Sonnet (the more capable model) does the deep work here. It identifies patterns across the trade history: which symbols have been working, which factors have been most predictive, whether the system is performing better on Day vs Swing trades, whether certain market regimes should be avoided. These insights can inform manual adjustments to the adaptive weights if the automated engine hasn't caught a pattern yet.

8. Adaptive Learning Engine

After every closed trade, the system automatically reviews the last 8 closed trades and adjusts three parameters: factor weights, signal threshold, and stop multiplier. No manual tuning is required — the engine finds what works in current market conditions and reinforces it, then backs off what is not working.

Factor Weight Adaptation
For each of the 9 factors, look at last 8 trades where that factor was present: Win rate > 70% → weight × 1.10 (capped at 1.5× default) Win rate < 40% → weight × 0.75 (floored at 0.30× default) Win rate 40–70% AND weight was reduced → weight × 1.05 (recovery) Example: if ADX >25 has been present in 7/8 wins → ADX weight: 1.0 → 1.1 → 1.21 → ... up to 1.5 Higher ADX weight means trending-market signals score higher → more fires Example: if VWAP has been present in 7/8 losses → VWAP weight: 1.0 → 0.75 → 0.56 → ... down to 0.30 Lower VWAP weight means VWAP alignment matters less in scoring

The weights represent the system's current empirical view of what matters. If the current market is range-bound and VWAP keeps being present on losing trades, the engine concludes that VWAP alignment is a false signal in this environment. When the market shifts back to trending, VWAP signals start winning again and the weight recovers. The system adapts to market regimes without being told they changed.

Signal Threshold Adaptation
Review: last 8 closed trades (wins + losses) Overall win rate < 40%: threshold += 0.5 (requires stronger setups) Overall win rate > 70%: threshold −= 0.25 (allows slightly lower bar) 3+ consecutive losses: threshold += 0.5 (streak protection, immediate) Range: 6.5 minimum (never too permissive) to 9.5 maximum (never impossible)

The threshold controls signal frequency. In a good run (70%+ win rate) it lowers slightly to catch more opportunities. In a losing streak it raises to force the system to be more selective — only the highest-conviction setups get through. The consecutive loss protection fires faster than the win-rate check: 3 losses in a row immediately tightens the bar, rather than waiting for the full 8-trade window.

Stop Multiplier Adaptation
Check last 5 losses: If 3+ of them had Stop within 0.5% of Entry: stop_multiplier += 0.1 (widens ATR buffer on new trades) Range: 0.3× ATR minimum → 1.0× ATR maximum Default: 0.5×ATR buffer below/above the structural stop level

If recent losses are being stopped out within 0.5% of entry, the market is printing wicks that just barely clip the stop before reversing in the original direction — a sign the stop buffer is too tight for current volatility. The system detects this and widens the buffer on future trades. As stops get wider, the R:R enforcement means targets are projected further too — the whole setup scales proportionally.

Viewing the Learning State

The /api/learning endpoint (and the Learning card on the dashboard) shows all current values: each factor's weight vs default, current signal threshold, stop multiplier, win rate, and the last 15 adaptation changes with timestamps. The adaptation log entries explain exactly what changed and why — e.g. "⬆ 'adx' weight 1.00→1.10 (present in 6/8 wins, win rate 75%)".

Timeframe Switcher

The TF buttons (15m / 1H / 4H / 1D / 1W) switch the chart view and re-run analysis for that timeframe. The scanner actively monitors 15m and 4H. The 1H, 1D, and 1W views are display-only — no trades are logged from them — but they show the higher-timeframe context used by the bias filter. Switching to 1H shows you exactly what the 15m bias filter is checking.
